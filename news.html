Indian Electorate:

Voting : Your Right


Here's why every citizen of India must cast his or her vote:

Scale: More than 814 million voters were eligible to cast ballots to elect the lower house of parliament, or Lok Sabha, up from 713 million voters in 2009.

Polling Stations:Total number of polling stations for 2014 General Elections were 9,19,452

EVMs Used: Total number of EVMs used in the 2014 General Elections were – 17,20,080 control units and 18,78,306 ballot units (2014)

Constituency Facts: Largest parliamentary constituency in terms of number of Electors is Malkajgiri, Andhra Pradesh (29,53,915) and smallest is Lakshwadeep (47,972)

Marking Ink used 20,140 litres of Unique Violet Ink was dispatched by Mysore Paints & Varnish Ltd (MVPL) for elections 2014

Party Participation:There are 6 national parties and all participated in Elections 2014. Out of 47 state parties 39 participated in the elections. Also, there are 1634 unrecognized parties out of which 419 participated in it

Candidature:Total number of Candidates in 543 seats – 8251 (7578 male, 668 female and 5 transgender candidates). Among the elected candidates – 482 are male and 61 female
